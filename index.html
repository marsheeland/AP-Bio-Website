    1.3 | Introduction to Macromolecules

    1.3 goes over hydrolysis & dehydration synthesis, the key take aways are:

  • Hydrolysis involves breaking apart molecules. It breaks the covalent bond, with the hydrogen ion and 2 oxygen ions releasing to make a hydrogen (hence the name)
  • Dehydration synthesis is the OPPOSITE to hydrolysis, it brings two molecules together to make a polymer through a covalent bond. It works the opposite to hydrolysis in every way.
  • Hydrolysis = Polymer -> Monomer. Dehydration Synthesis = Monomer -> Polymer

    1.5 | Lipids

    1.5 goes over lipds, this is when it starts getting complicated what you need to know regarding lipids is that:

  • Typically NONpolar, hydrophobic, structure & function derived from arrangement of their subcomponents. Capable of being Unsaturated or saturated.
  • Saturated has one bond between carbon atoms, is solid at room temperature, and has no kink in the tail.
  • Unsaturated has AT LEAST one double bond between carbon atoms, causing a kink in the tail of the carbon chain. It is liquid at room temperature.
  • Fat provide LONG term energy storage & support cell fucntion. Can provide insulation for mammals.
  • Steroids are lipids: Supports physiological functions (like growth, development), metabolism, & homeostatsis.
  • Cholesterol provides structural stability to animal cell membranes
  • PHOSPHOlipids (phospo = phosphate) group together to make liquid bilayers in plasma/cell membranes.
  • REMEMBER! think unsaturated = liquid, twisted tail ; saturated = solid, straight tail
    • Timestamp: 14:00 - 17:45

    1.6 | Nucleic Acids

    1.6 goes over nucleic acids (DNA/RNA IS COMPOSED OF THIS, REMEMBER!!) *I ADVISE SIMPLY GOING OVER UNIT 6 INSTEAD*

  • Nucleic acids (DNA/RNA) are encoded in sequences of NUCLEOTIDE monomers. Nucleotides are made of: 5-carbon sugar (deoxyribose/ribose), phosphate, & nitrogenous base (A,C,T,G)
  • linear sequence of nucleotides that have ends with a 3 prime hydroxyl and 5 prime phosphates of the usugar in phosphates of the sugar. in nucleic acid synthesis, nulceotides added to 3' end resulting in covalent bonds between nucleotides.
  • DNA has deoxyribose sugar (no oxygen), RNA has ribose sugar (has oxygen). DNA has thymine, RNA has uracil. DNA is double stranded, RNA is single stranded.
